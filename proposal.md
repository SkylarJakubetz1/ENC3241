# Proposal

Optimizing Plant Growth Through Sensor Feedback

Skylar Jakubetz Electrical Engineering

Background
Indoor plants have grown in popularity because of their positive contribution to a
peaceful environment and fresh oxygen (Wells, 2023). However, maintaining optimal soil
moisture remains a significant challenge. Traditional irrigation methods are flawed and don’t
measure realistic plant environments, including humidity and temperature changes. Manual
irrigation methods tend to increase the risk of overwatering and underwatering, promoting water
mold in stems, which reduces growth (PlantIn, 2022). To ensure accurate moisture regulations
and the reduction of plant stress, microcontrollers like Raspberry Pi offer a low-cost solution to
plant care. Research shows that automated irrigation systems are more efficient than traditional
methods. UC Agriculture and Natural Resources says, “Traditional irrigation methods often lead
to water waste due to overwatering and evaporation losses” (Lofton, 2025). With advances in the
Internet of Things (IoT), smart watering systems can use soil moisture sensors to monitor soil
moisture, humidity, and temperature to deliver water to the plant when necessary (Mottech,
2025). This research project is personally relevant because I am interested in sustainable
solutions for everyday life.

Methodology

The data for this research project will be collected through direct measurements, system logging,
and user feedback during a controlled plant growth experiment.
First, the Raspberry Pi 4 will be programmed to monitor soil moisture and water plants
when moisture levels fall below a certain threshold. Periodically, the water will be pumped into
the plant's soil using a 3-5V micro submersible water pump with a clear vinyl PVC tubing (1M).
Soil moisture will be monitored through a capacitive analog soil moisture sensor, 3.3-5.5V.
To gather data, there will be six control groups that are manually watered, and six
experimental groups using the automated watering system. Six Calathea plants will be treated
with a standard irrigation schedule. Given 150mL of room temperature water every 72 hours. Six
Calathea plants will be watered based on the Raspberry Pi feedback from the sensor in the soil.
Over a 7-week timeline, soil moisture, water usage, frequency, and growth will be logged
and organized into tables and graphs. Growth will be assessed bi-weekly by measuring height
(cm) and leaf count.
